Purpose

This annotation aims to label the chats from the game live streaming chatroom that contains offensive statements. Meanwhile, targeting offensive language and labeling its type helps us better understand the language's characteristics and the sender's intent, thus creating a safe and civilized online environment for users.

Definition

  • Offensive language employs profanity, is strongly impolite, rude or vulgar red expressed with fighting or hurtful words to insult a targeted individual or group.

Targets of offensive language:

  • Individual: Posts targeting an individual. It can be a famous person, a named individual or an unnamed participant in the conversation. Insults/threats targeted at individuals are often defined as cyberbullying.
  • Group: The target of these offensive posts is a group of people considered as a unity due to the same ethnicity, gender or sexual orientation, political affiliation, religious belief, or other common characteristics. Many of the insults and threats targeted at a group correspond to what is commonly understood as hate speech.
  • Others: The target of these offensive posts does not belong to any of the previous two categories, e.g., an organization, a situation, an event, or an issue.

Types of offensive language:

  • Racism: Prejudice, discrimination, or antagonism directed against other people because they are of a different race or ethnicity.
  • Sexism: Prejudice or discrimination based on a person's sex or gender. It has been linked to stereotypes and gender roles, and may include the belief that one sex or gender is intrinsically superior to another.
  • Personal attack: Making of an abusive remark on or relating to one's person instead of providing evidence when examining another person's claims or comments.
  • Others: Does not fall into one of the above three categories, or is difficult or impossible to categorize.

Principle

Each message has up to three labels:

  • Is it offensive?
    • OFF for offensive
    • NOT for not offensive

    * If a message is non-English or more than half of the words are not recognizable, annotate it as Unreadable and skip the following annotation.

  • Does it has a target? (if it is offensive)
    • IND for individual
    • GRP for a group of people
    • OTH for others
    • NOT for has no target
  • What type is it? (if it is offensive)
    • RAC for racism
    • SEX for sexism
    • PAT for personal attack
    • OTH for others

    * If a message multiple types, such as

    Stfu bitch ass nigga

    use this order: Racism > Sexism > Personal attack > Others. Therefore, the above example will be annotated as RAC.
